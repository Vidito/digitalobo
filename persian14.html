reminder:
requsts or commands:
boro! go!
bia! come!
bekhor! eat!
we add 'be' to the beginning of verbs to make commands or requests
now look at these examples:
where shall I go? what shall I eat? shall I go? shall we eat?
here we are asking for orders, or advice, or suggestions, so the structure is the same, now look at the ending
kojaa biaam? where should I come? --> bia khoone!
chi bekhoram? what shall i eat? ---> pizza bekhor!
kojaa beram? where shall i go? ---> boro khoone
ghazaa bekhorim? shall we eat? ---> aare, bekhorim


the verb 'to do' = kardan
lots of verbs in Persian are made with the combination of "kardan". for instance 'to play' = baazi kardan (literally 'to do game')
to listen = goosh kardan
to paint = rang kardan
to work = kaar kardan
to live = zendegi kardan.
conjugaion:
I play = man baazi mikonam.
you listen = to goosh mikoni.
he works = oo kaar mikone
we live = maa zendegi mikonim
they paint = oona rang mikonan.
To form and imperative (command) as we learnt before, we we normally add 'be' to the beginning of a verb (without the 'mi' part):
baazi bekon! Play!
however for this verb it is not necessary to add the 'be', so
baazi kon! Play.
rang kon! Paint!
kaar kon! Work!


more examples:
aasemoon (sky) ro rang kon! paint the sky!
in naghaashi (painting) ro rang kon! color this painting!
tooye khoone kaar kon! work at home!

Reading Comprehension

The object marker 'raa' / 'ro' / 'o'
the words in bold are direct objects
I see John. man John ro mibninam. man John-o mibinam
I give the book to John. man ketaab ro (ketaab-o) be John midam.
I eat the cake. man keik ro (keik-o) mikhoram
Note that whenever you are not talking a specific thing or person, you don't need to use 'ro'
I am buying a book. man ketab mikharam.
I watch a film. man film mibinam.
I drink tea. man chai mikhoram

Let's translate

I want to buy THE book.
I like to see Albert.
you should eat (food).
Note that you don't need 'ro' when you use words like 'to' or 'on' as in 'listen to music', 'work on a project'
I work on a project: .....................
I listen to music.


Conversation
John: mikhai beri biroon?
Albert: na, tuye khoone mimoonam (stay)
John: mikhai che kar bekoni?
Albert: mikham darsam ro bekhoonam. to chetor?
John: man farda darsam ro mikhoonam. alaan mikham beram sinemaa.
Albert: ok, khosh begzare (have fun).

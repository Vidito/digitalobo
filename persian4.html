An Introduction to the Present Tense in Persian

Present simple in Farsi has the same concept as in English. I drink tea, I sleep at 10, I eat lunch at 1,...
To use the present tense, we simply use the prefix 'mi-'+the root of the verb+the proper ending for different persons:


# Persian English
man mi-khaab-am I sleep
to mi-khaab-i You sleep
1 oo mi-khaab-e S/He sleeps
2 maa mi-khaab-im we sleep
3 shomaa mi-khaab-id you (plural) sleep
Let's put an object man aab mi-khor-am I drink water
5 to be madrese mi-r-i you go to school
5 oo ketaab mi-khoon-e He reads a book
6 ma dars mi-khoon-im we study lessons
MORE EXAMPLES
1 shomaa naahaar mi-khor-id You eat lunch

Note:

You have noticed that the beginning and the ending for these verbs is the same. Now this is the case for most one word verbs. It is not that easy though to learn the root of all the verbs. It just takes practice and repetition.



Questions

In Persian, to make wh-questions, we simply put the question word where the answer word should be: he eats at 10==> he eats when?

# Persian English
1 chi mi-khor-i? (shokolaat) What do you eat? (chocolate)
1 kojaa mi-r-e? (khoone) where is he going? (home)
2 key mi-khaab-i? (saa'at do) when do you sleep? (at 2)

Can you translate?

I go to school at 1.

They drink pepsi.

They sleep at night.

For making negative sentences we simply add "ne" to the beginning of the verbs
man ne-mikhaabam ==> I don't sleep

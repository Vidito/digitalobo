An Introduction to Telling the time, How many/How much and Plural



# Persian English
saa'at hour/watch/clock
chand how many
1 saa'at chand-e? What time is it?
2 saa'at yek-e It's one o'clock
3 saa'at panj-e it's five o'clock
4 daghighe minute
5 saa'at yek o panj daghighe ast it's one five
5 saa'at yek o rob-e it's a quarter past one
6 saa'at yek o nim-e It's half past one
MORE EXAMPLES
1 saa'at 5 daghighe be yek-e it's five minutes to one

Note:

after numbers we don't make plural nouns:
5 minutes => panj daghighe (daghighe is singular)
instead of 'saa'at yek o panj daghighe-ast', you can also say : saa'at yek o panj daghighe-e)



Plural

In Persian, referring to several instances of something is done by using the number+ taa+ singular noun:
ye ketaab / do taa ketaab / panj taa ketaab / dah taa ketaab

# Persian English
1 to chand taa ketaab daar-i? how many books do you have?
1 man ye ketaab daaram I have one book
2 man panj taa ketaab daaram I have five books

Can you translate?

He has two school bags.

They have three white books.



Plural

We add plural marks 'aa or haa' to the end of most nouns to make plural nouns as in "these houses, whose books?,... Remember that we use the singular forms when nouns are immediately preceded by numbers or words such as several, a few,...

# Persian English
ketaab-aa books
1 ketaab-aa zir-e miz-an the books are under the desk
2 bache-haa tu-ye khoone-an the children are in the house
3 holandi-aa mehraboon-an the Dutch people are kind

Note that THESE and THOSE when use independently are 'in-aa' and 'oon-aa' (like the plural form of 'in' and 'oon'), but when used before a plural noun, they are used in singular form:
in ketaab-aa aabi-an
oon film-aa khoob-an

Translate

They are my books

My teachers are good

Those tables are black

These are boys

I have green cars

The books are here (HERE means injaa)

boys and girls

Prepositions
When using the prepositions, we add ‘e’ or ‘ye’ to the prepositions if they are followed by a noun.
On the table = roo-ye miz
Under the table = zir-e miz
Examples:
Ketaab kojaa-e? or ketaab kojaast?
Ketaab, zir-e miz-e.
Ketaabaa kojaan?
Ketaabaa zir-e miz-an
To koja-i?
Oo kojaast?
Maashin-et kojaast?

Dard = pain / dast-am = my hand VS dastaam = my hands/ paam = my leg or foot VS paahaam = my legs or feet / saram = my head
Saram dard mikone = my head hurts /
Paam dard mikone
Paahaam dard mikon-an
Chap = left / raast = right
Dast-e chap = left hand
Paa-ye raast = right leg
Moch-e paa = ankle
Moch-e dast = wrist

Subjenctive

Look at the following phrases:
I go: man mi-ram
I want : mi-khaam
I like : doost daaram,
I can : mi-toonam I should : man baayad
I may : momkene (literally, it is possible)

Now Look at the following sentences in English:
I want to go: man mikhaaam beram.
I like to go: man doost daaram beram
I should go: man baayad beram
I may go: man momkene beram
I can go: man mitoonam beram

All of them express desire, intention, obligation, or possibility.
In these cases, the first verb is normally conjugated as the phrases above followed by the second ver starting with 'be'
more examples:
man mittonam khoone beram.
man mikham ghazaa (food) bekhoram.
man baayad zood (soon) beram.

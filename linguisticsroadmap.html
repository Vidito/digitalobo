The roadmap to linguistics

  • by:VAHID NIAMADPOUR PhD RESEARCHER
  • date:April 10 , 2020

This is the path you can take if you are interested in linguistics. Starting off with general introductory books, you will find which area you are most interested in. Thereafter, you can find it down the list and see which resources are the best to take. The resources are the ones that I have used throughout my linguistics journey (more than 14 years), so they have been tried and tested. Some have been revised and edited many times and some have been recently published. Besides the first stage which is “General introductory Linguistics books”, there is no specific order to the fields or books. Feel free to send me your suggestions and I will add them to list.

Limits of my laguage mean the limits of my world. -Ludwig Wittgenstein

General introductory Linguistics books:

The study of language by George Yule
An Introduction to Language by Fromkin and Rodman
An Introduction to language and linguistics by Fasold

Phonetics and Phonology:

English Phonetics and Phonology by Peter roach

Morphology:

Introducing Morphology by Rochelle Lieber

Syntax:

Understanding Syntax (Understanding Language) by Maggie Tallerman.
For those interested in systemic functional linguistics:

  • Halliday's Introduction to Functional Grammar by Michael Halliday
  • A Systemic Functional Grammar of English: A Simple Introduction by David banks
For those interested in Chomskyan linguistics:
  • Chomsky's Universal Grammar: An Introduction by Vivian Cook, Mark Newson
  • Analysing English Sentences by Andre Radford

Semantics:

Semantics: A Coursebook by James Hurford

Pragmatics:

Understanding Pragmatics by Jef Verschueren
Pragmatics (Routledge English Language Introductions) by Joan Cutting

Discourse:

Discourse Analysis: An Introduction by Brian Paltridg
How to do Discourse Analysis: A Toolkit by James Paul Gee
Discourse: A Critical Introduction (Key Topics in Sociolinguistics) by Jan Blommaert

Critical Discourse Analysis (CDA):

The Practice of Critical Discourse Analysis: an Introduction by Meriel Bloor , Thomas Bloor
Critical Discourse Analysis: The Critical Study of Language by Norman Fairclough
How to Do Critical Discourse Analysis: A Multimodal Introduction by David Machin, Andrea Mayr

Sociolinguistics:

An Introduction to Sociolinguistics (Learning about Language) by Janet Holmes and Nick Wilson

Neurolinguistics:

Introduction to Neurolinguistics By Elisabeth Ahlsén

Historical Linguistics:

Trask's Historical Linguistics by Robert McColl Millar

Psycholinguistics:

Psycholinguistics: The Key Concepts (Routledge Key Guides) by John Field
Introducing Psycholinguistics by Paul Warren

Language ad Culture:

Language and Culture by Claire Kramsch

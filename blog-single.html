THE 3 BEST LINGUISTICS BOOKS FOR BEGINNERS

  • by:VAHID NIAMADPOUR PhD RESEARCHER
  • date:April 9, 2020

So, you have realised that you are interested in languages and how they work, are learnt, are taught, are used in different contexts, are used to persuade people, 'talk' them into doing something, exercise power, .... .

You google "what is the best introduction to language?" or "what is the best linguistics textbook?" and come up with thousands of results. Which one do you pick? As a linguist, I have reviewed the best introductory linguistics books there are in the market which will equip you with a solid foundation of what linguistics is and how it is applied to real world issues

Limits of my laguage mean the limits of my world. -Ludwig Wittgenstein

1- The Study of Language By George Yule 7th ed.

The best first introduction ever! Invincible! This book is by far the most accessible and user-friendly introdction to the field of linguisics and also my first exposure to linguistics.
It not only covers 'core' areas such as syntax and phonology, but also the origins of language, pragmatics, discourse analysis, historical linguistics and more. Each chapter succeeds in providing a thorough overview of its topic while remaining at a level appropriate to newcomers in the field. The book is written in a style accessible even to non-native English speakers. The study questions and tasks at the end of each chapter are engaging and the discussion topics/projects are designed to push students beyond the material, and to use their own experiences and analytical skills to think about language.

Buy The Study of Language (English Edition)

2- An Introduction to Language by Fromkin, Rodman, and Hyams 11th ed

11th edition! Wow that says a lot about this book. I came across this book almost ten years ago. Having read George Yule's study of language , this was the best next book in line for me.
Besides the core areas of linguistics (sounds, semantics, morphology, syntax), it covers 'language in society', 'language change', 'language acquisition', and 'language processing and himan brain'.
This book is unbelievably valuable for getting to know major areas of linguistics and since no linguistics introduction can do away with Chomsky's ideas, this one does it in a very good and "undull" way.

Buy An Introduction to Language by Fromkin

3- An Introduction to language and Linguistics by Fasold

Now, this brings us to the last and most detailed book written by several experts in the field.
This textbook addresses the full scope of language, from the traditional subjects of structural linguistics (relating to sound, form, meaning and language change) to the more specialised subjects of contextual linguistics (including discourse, dialect variation, language and culture, and the politics of language). There are also separate chapters on language and the brain, computational linguistics, writing, and first and second language learning.
each chapter has been written by an expert in the field, so you can be sure that they know exactly what they are talking about.

Buy An Introduction to Language and Linguistics

Conclusion

The list above includes all the necessary introductory linguistics books you can find on the market to get you familiar with almost all areas of linguistics. From there, you can see which area appeals to you more than the others and focus on that specific area.
You might find yourself interested in Pragmatics, or Sociolinguistics, or Syntax. What you can do then is read my review of the books for each of those fields which aim for people who have already learned the basics. The links above are affiliate links to amazon.
